# Rinkd Scorer Guide

**As a scorer, you run the live scoreboard during games — logging goals, penalties, and shots from rinkside so the standings and feed update in real time.**

You'll do this on an iPad using the Rinkd Scorer View. No app store download needed — it's a web app.

---

## Before game day

### Getting added by a director

The tournament director will add you to the tournament by entering your @handle or email address in the Scorers tab of the tournament settings. Once they do:

- If they used your email, you'll receive an email invitation.
- If they used your @handle, you'll see a notification in Rinkd.

Accept the invitation (or simply sign in to Rinkd on game day — your access will be ready).

You do NOT need to be the director to run scores. You just need a Rinkd account and to be listed as a scorer on the tournament.

## Setting up your iPad

1. Open Safari on the iPad.
2. Go to **rinkd.app**.
3. Sign in to your Rinkd account.
4. Leave the browser open — you'll navigate to each game as the day starts.

> **Tip:** The iPad screen will stay on automatically while Scorer View is open. If your device is too old to support that (you'll see a warning banner), just adjust your screen auto-lock setting manually before the first game.

[screenshot: Rinkd sign-in on iPad Safari]

## Starting a game

1. From the home screen of Rinkd, tap **Tournaments**.
2. Find the tournament and tap it to open it.
3. Tap the **Schedule** tab.
4. Find the game you're about to score.
5. Tap **Open Scorer View** on that game.

The Scorer View loads. You'll see the two team names, a score display (starting 0–0), and controls for goals, penalties, shots, and the period.

6. When the puck drops, tap the **+** button next to the home team or away team to add the first goal, OR tap the period selector to move to **1st period** — either action starts the game clock running in the system.

[screenshot: Scorer View overview — score display, team names, period selector]

---

## Logging a goal

1. Tap **Log Goal** (or the **+** goal button for the team that scored).
2. A form slides up from the bottom. Fill in:
   - **Team** — choose which team scored (it pre-fills based on which + you tapped).
   - **Scorer jersey #** — type the jersey number of the player who scored. Leave blank if unknown.
   - **Assist 1 jersey #** — type the first assisting player's number. Optional.
   - **Assist 2 jersey #** — type the second assisting player's number. Optional.
   - **Period** — which period the goal happened in.
   - **Time in period** — the clock time when the goal was scored (e.g., `7:22`). Optional but useful for the scoresheet.
3. Tap **Save**.

The score on the scoreboard updates immediately. The goal appears in the goal log below.

> **Made a mistake?** Tap the goal in the log and tap **Delete**. The score adjusts automatically.

[screenshot: Log Goal form with jersey # fields]

---

## Logging a penalty

1. Tap **Add Penalty**.
2. Fill in:
   - **Team** — which team is taking the penalty.
   - **Player jersey #** — the number of the player penalized. Optional.
   - **Penalty type** — choose from the list (Hooking, Slashing, Roughing, etc.).
   - **Severity** — Minor (2 min), Double Minor (4 min), Major (5 min), Game Misconduct, or Match Penalty.
   - **Period** and **Time in period** — when it happened.
3. Tap **Save**.

The penalty appears in the penalty log.

[screenshot: Add Penalty form]

## Tracking shots on goal

Shots are optional but help with the scoresheet.

1. Find the **Shots** row for each team on the Scorer View screen.
2. Tap **+** to add a shot for the home team or the away team.
3. Shots are tracked per period — the count resets each time you advance the period.

[screenshot: shots row with + and − buttons per team]

---

## Switching periods

1. Find the period selector near the top of the Scorer View.
2. Tap the period you are moving to: **2nd**, **3rd**, **OT**, etc.

The system updates the current period. The period selector only shows the periods your tournament format allows — if your tournament goes straight to a shootout with no overtime, the OT button won't appear.

[screenshot: period selector showing 1st 2nd 3rd Final]

---

## Finalizing a game

When the final buzzer sounds:

1. Tap **Final** on the period selector.
2. A confirmation dialog may appear if your score and goal log don't match — review and confirm.
3. For a **tied bracket game** (semifinals, finals), a **Shootout Winner** prompt will appear before you can finalize. Tap the team that won the shootout.
4. Tap **Finalize**.

As soon as the game is finalized:

- The standings update automatically for everyone watching.
- A game recap chirp is posted to the tournament's Feed tab.
- Everyone who follows the tournament receives a push notification with the final score.

The Scorer View switches to read-only. You cannot make changes after finalizing.

[screenshot: Final period selector and Shootout Winner prompt]

---

## What to do if you finalized too early

Once a game is finalized, **only the tournament director can reopen it.** You will see a message in Scorer View that says "Only the director can reopen."

Contact the director immediately. They can tap **🔓 Reopen Game** from their Scorer View, which unlocks the game for editing. Once they reopen it, you'll be able to make corrections. Finalize again when everything is right.

[screenshot: finalized game banner with Reopen button visible to directors]

## Quick reference

| What to do | How |
|---|---|
| Open Scorer View | Tournaments → Schedule → tap game → Open Scorer View |
| Log a goal | Tap Log Goal → fill in team + jersey # → Save |
| Delete a goal | Tap the goal in the log → Delete |
| Log a penalty | Tap Add Penalty → fill in details → Save |
| Track shots | Tap + next to each team in the Shots row |
| Switch periods | Tap the period on the period selector |
| Finalize a game | Tap Final → confirm → Finalize |
| Reopen a game | Director only — contact the director |
