## Outreach playbook (lessons locked in — apply to every email)

- **Slow-walk. Don't come in hot.** First touch is a light, peer-to-peer hello with a soft, no-pressure ask ("show you sometime, get your take") — NOT the full pitch + big ask. Save the big asks (ambassador, partnership) for touch #2/#3 once there's rapport. (The "Iron & Steel" mistake = came in too hot.)
- **Give-first.** Lead with value/genuine interest, not "buy my thing." For charity events, offer to help **free** so more goes to the cause.
- **Never be "stalky."** Do NOT name their backend stack in the email (no "I see you use ActiveNet/EventConnect/HockeyShift…"). Reference only public, surface-level things ("saw your 3v3 series"). Naming their internals signals you dug through their systems.
- **Additive wedge, not rip-and-replace.** Position Rinkd as the **game-day + social/community layer that runs alongside** their existing registration — "keep what you use for sign-ups." Rinkd is NOT a registration tool yet.
- **Lead with the social angle** (feed, recaps, chirps) **+ game features** (live scoring, standings, schedules) **+ the before/during/after engagement loop.** That's the differentiator vs every incumbent below — none of them are social/community platforms.
- **Brand voice:** hockey-native, plainspoken, warm, confident-not-cocky; dial chirp DOWN for serious contexts (e.g. veterans charity). "Rinkd" styling.
- **Send-from:** `pete@rinkd.app` (Claude can't send email). Swap "Hi there" for a real name when available.

Matt runs **24 events/season**, uses GameSheet ($15/team) + Regystra (1%), stance is *"my process isn't broken."* Three lines that closed the room: (1) the **social layer** on top, (2) **before / during / after** engagement loop, (3) ⭐ **"52-week engagement vs one-weekend transactions."** Matt's own ask back: *"can I try it as just the community/engagement piece without disrupting what I have?"* Resulting build: **`SOCIAL-1` — GameSheet sidecar import + tournament "social-only" mode** (handoff §7). **Lesson reinforced:** for high-volume nimble operators on rented stacks (GameSheet / Regystra / LeagueApps / EventConnect), the wedge IS the deal; resist the urge to pitch replacement.

- **Distribution** through Rinkd's channels + tournament-partner networks (BLPA, Hockey Against Hate, etc.).
- **Official Rinkd Media Credentials** — lets student writers request press access to junior teams (USHL/NAHL), NCAA programs, tournaments. Costs nothing to issue; worth a lot to a 20-year-old breaking into sports media.
