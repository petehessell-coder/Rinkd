# 01 — VISION (the North Star document)

## Mission
Become **the social layer for all of hockey.**

We are NOT replacing SportsEngine. We are NOT replacing Crossbar. We are NOT
replacing GameSheet, TeamSnap, or LeagueApps. **We connect them.**

- Every hockey participant should have a **Rinkd profile** (their hockey identity).
- Every team should have a **Rinkd page**.
- Every game should have a **Rinkd experience** (live, recap, POTG/Game Puck, share).
- Every league should have a **Rinkd community**.
- Every tournament should have a **Rinkd home**.

## Why this shape wins (the Platform Layer Play)
The ops/registration/scoring platforms own the "back of house" (registration,
scheduling, payments, scorekeeping). **Nobody owns the front of house** — the fan
and player engagement layer. That is the wedge, and it is also the exit path. Rinkd
arrives because *your team or your event is already on Rinkd*, synced from the
platform the operator already uses (GameSheet, HockeyShift, EventConnect…). We
increase the value of the operator's existing stack instead of threatening it.

One platform partnership = a whole operator cluster of teams, leagues, and
tournaments. That is how a fan layer scales from one rink to all of hockey.

## The two pillars (already built — we elevate, not rebuild)
**1. Rinkd for associations** (tournaments / teams / leagues): registration,
scheduling, rosters, stats, standings, team/league/tournament comms, real-time
scoring, player profile pages.

**2. Rinkd for fans:** team/league/tournament feeds, follow users/players/teams,
photo galleries, live stat & standings updates, merch store, direct links to live
game streams.

## The scale ceiling we design for
Rinkd's ceiling is not a single rink. A single large operator (e.g. Black Bear: 50
rinks, 1,000+ teams) plus two more at that scale = tens of thousands of concurrent
users on a Saturday night. Every decision must hold at that load. See the Saturday
Night Test in `CLAUDE.md` and `00_MASTER_PROMPT.md`.

## What we are NOT
- Not "another sports management app." The ops tooling is table stakes; the
  engagement layer is the product.
- Not "Instagram for hockey" to an operator's face — that framing scares partners
  and reads as a consumer toy. To operators we are the **engagement layer that
  makes their platform stickier**. To fans and players we feel like the social app
  they already know how to use.
- Not a place that ever exposes a minor's personal information publicly.

## The one line that keeps us honest
> Never make a commissioner feel like they're doing office work. Never make a parent
> feel like they're using enterprise software.
